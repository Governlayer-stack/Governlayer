The Story

I saw the governance gap before it became a crisis

In 2022, I joined Google to lead security, risk, and compliance programs across the most consequential AI products in the world — Gemini, DeepMind, Waymo, Vertex AI, and Google Cloud. What I found was a familiar pattern: mature compliance infrastructure built for traditional software, but nothing designed for the unique risks of AI systems.

There was no framework for behavioral drift. No standard for training data provenance. No certification process for generative AI products. The EU AI Act was coming, ISO 42001 had just been published, and the industry was about to ship autonomous AI agents with no governance rails.

So I built it. At Google, I designed the company's first AI Management System. Outside of Google, I founded GovernLayer to make that infrastructure available to every organization deploying AI. And I started AI LAX Summit to bring the ecosystem together — founders, enterprise leaders, and policymakers — because governance isn't a solo effort.

What I'm building

G

GovernLayerAutonomous AI Governance Platform

GovernLayer provides real-time compliance, security, and risk management infrastructure for enterprises deploying AI agents. The platform continuously monitors AI systems for behavioral drift, policy violations, bias, and security vulnerabilities — enforcing governance decisions through an immutable, hash-chained audit ledger.

Built for the agentic AI era, where autonomous systems make decisions at machine speed and traditional compliance frameworks can't keep up. The platform covers 9 compliance frameworks, runs bias detection and prompt injection scanning, orchestrates 14 LLM models for hallucination-resistant analysis, and generates audit-ready evidence automatically.

AI LAX Summit is a Los Angeles-based convening organization bringing together AI founders, enterprise leaders, and policymakers to advance responsible AI deployment.
