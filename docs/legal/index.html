Purpose

GovernLayer is inviting you to participate in a limited beta program for our AI governance platform. This agreement sets expectations for both parties during the beta testing period.

2

Beta Period

  • Start Date: To be determined upon mutual agreement
  • End Date: Approximately 1 week from start
  • Either party may terminate participation at any time with written notice (email is sufficient).
3

What You Get

Full access to the GovernLayer platform including:

  • AI compliance auditing (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001)
  • Behavioral drift detection
  • Risk scoring (6-dimension analysis)
  • Hash-chained audit ledger
  • Multi-LLM consensus engine
  • REST API and dashboard access

In addition, you receive:

  • Direct communication channel with the GovernLayer engineering team
  • Priority consideration for feature requests
  • No charge during the beta period
4

What We Ask of You

  • Use the platform with representative (non-production) data where possible
  • Provide honest feedback on functionality, usability, and value
  • Report bugs or unexpected behavior promptly
  • Complete a brief exit survey at the end of the beta period
  • Participate in at least one feedback call during the beta

Important: The beta platform should not be used as your sole compliance or governance tool. It is intended for evaluation purposes.
6

Data Handling

  • Your Data: You retain full ownership of all data you submit to the platform.
  • Storage: Data is stored in encrypted PostgreSQL databases hosted on Railway (US infrastructure).
  • Access: Only GovernLayer engineering staff may access your data, solely for debugging and platform improvement.
  • Deletion: Upon request or at the end of the beta period, we will delete all your data within 14 business days.
  • No Selling: We will never sell, share, or monetize your data.
Sensitive Data: Do not submit real personally identifiable information (PII), protected health information (PHI), or production credentials during the beta. Use anonymized or synthetic data.

Exception: Either party may share that a beta testing relationship exists (e.g., "We are evaluating GovernLayer" or "Company X is a beta tester"), but not details of findings, features, or data.
